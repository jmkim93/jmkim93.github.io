Hi there, welcome to my page.

My name is Jungmin Kim and I am a postdoctoral researcher at the University of Wisconsin-Madison working with Prof. Zongfu Yu. I'm interested in optical and photonic simulations and their applications to optical computing technology. Before joining here, I completed my doctoral study at Seoul National University in South Korea under the guidance of Prof. Namkyoo Park and Prof. Sunkyu Yu. During my PhD, I specialized in the numerical analysis of various optical systems, such as photonic crystals and disordered systems, especially in terms of band and spectral engineering.

Outside of the lab, I enjoy running, hiking, and taking road trips. Since moving to the United States, I have explored Ontario, Utah, DC as well as neighboring states and cities including South Dakota, Minnesota, Illinois, Iowa, and of course Wisconsin. While I really love the relaxed, lakeside life in Madison, WI, I also aim to visit as many wilderness areas within US national parks as possible and all of 50 states for my own Bucket list.
